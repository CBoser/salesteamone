FINDING 2: RICHMOND ELEVATION TRIPLE-ENCODING PROBLEM
------------------------------------------------------------
Pack: |10.82BCD OPT DEN FOUNDATION - ELVB - ELVC - ELVD

Elevation encoded THREE times:
  1. Pack ID suffix: "10.82BCD" 
  2. Location string: "- ELVB - ELVC - ELVD"
  3. Option codes: ELVB, ELVC, ELVD

This creates:
  ‚ùå Maintenance burden (update 3 places)
  ‚ùå Risk of inconsistency
  ‚ùå Violates DRY principle
  ‚ùå Makes queries complex

Solution needed in database design:
  ‚úÖ Pack definition: "10.82 OPT DEN FOUNDATION"
  ‚úÖ Elevation availability: B, C, D (separate field)
  ‚úÖ Job selection: Customer's chosen elevation


FINDING 3: PLAN NAMING INCONSISTENCY
------------------------------------------------------------

DECISION 1: Plan-Pack Relationship
------------------------------------------------------------
Question: When pack "12.x5" (2-car garage extension) appears on multiple 
          plans, is it the same pack or plan-specific?

Evidence needed:
  - Check if materials/quantities identical across plans
  - Example: Does pack 12.x5 on G603 have same materials as on G914?

Options:
  A) Universal Pack: One pack works on all plans
     ‚Üí pack_id alone is primary key
  
  B) Plan-Specific Pack: Pack varies by plan
     ‚Üí (plan_id, pack_id) is primary key

Implication: Affects database schema, affects data volume


DECISION 2: Plan-Elevation Model
------------------------------------------------------------
Question: Is "G603B" one thing, or is it Plan G603 + Elevation B?

Evidence:
  - Richmond G603 sheet contains all elevations (A, B, C)
  - Richmond LE93 G603B sheet is single elevation
  - Holt 1670ABCD CR sheet has all elevations

Options:
  A) Elevation as Plan Variant
     ‚Üí plan_id = "G603B" (elevation embedded)
  
  B) Elevation as Dimension
     ‚Üí plan_id = "G603", elevation = "B"

Implication: How to query "all elevations of plan X"
